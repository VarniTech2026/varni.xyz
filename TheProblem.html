The hidden cost

The cost that never appears on a technology budget

It lives in your operations team. It is the hours spent every day taking orders that arrived as PDFs, emails, and portal exports — and manually translating them into your ERP.

It is the errors that come from that translation. The rework that follows. The delayed processing. The dependency on the two or three people who know how to handle the exceptions.

For a manufacturing or distribution business processing hundreds of orders per week, the labor cost of manual order intake is significant — and it scales with volume. Every new customer, every new channel, every peak season makes it worse.

  • Orders arriving as PDFs, emails, and portal exports
  • Teams manually keying data into the ERP every day
  • Errors creeping in from rekeying and inconsistent formats
  • Limited visibility once orders are received
  • Processes depending on a handful of individuals
  • Batch integrations slowing down order flow
  • Volume peaks creating backlogs and team strain
  • Every new customer or channel makes the problem worse
